### 8. Performed First Error Analysis

- Re-ran the multichannel setting with prediction export as `artifacts/exp_with_multichannel_error_analysis/`.
- Located two concrete mistakes in the 16-sample test set:
  - false positive: `data/multichannel_kc_adm/ck-19-KC-adm-IF-40x.tif`
  - false negative: `data/KPC/1-amylase.tif`
- Interpreted both mistakes as evidence that channel-specific views should not be flattened into independent image samples.
- Concluded that the current main modeling bottleneck is sample organization, not backbone size.

## Main Conclusions Of The Day

- The repository now has a reproducible v1 training baseline under `src/`.
- The current folder labels are sufficient to support local baseline experiments.
- End-to-end backbone fine-tuning is necessary for this task; freezing the backbone hurts specificity badly.
- Multi-channel and multi-magnification images need lesion-level grouped modeling instead of ordinary image-level flattening.
- The next real accuracy improvement is more likely to come from lesion linkage, better split metadata, and targeted hard negatives than from a slightly more complex single-image architecture.
